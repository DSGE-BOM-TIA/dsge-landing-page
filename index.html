About DSGE

DSGE is being built as a deployment-ready operating system for circular recovery, traceable material flow, stakeholder coordination, and measurable value creation. The intent is to help organizations move from fragmented disposal-driven behavior toward structured recovery, resilience, and scalable economic impact.

Mission

Deploy practical circular systems that convert waste, underused material streams, and operational friction into measurable economic, environmental, and community value.

Vision

Build a scalable model that helps facilities, partners, institutions, and communities operate with stronger visibility, faster deployment capability, workforce readiness, and long-term circular resilience.

Values

  • Operational clarity over confusion
  • Measured value over vague claims
  • Preparedness over reaction
  • Partnership over fragmentation
  • Training and advancement over short-term labor extraction
  • Scalable systems over one-off fixes

Technology + Training Pathways

DSGE is intended to grow into a technology-enabled operating platform supported by practical field deployment, digital traceability, reporting workflows, material intelligence, and operator-friendly tools. Training pathways are designed to support entry, intermediate, and advanced capability development.

  • Operator onboarding and field procedures
  • Material identification, handling, and documentation
  • Traceability, chain-of-custody, and reporting support
  • Equipment operation and deployment readiness
  • Data, dashboards, and continuous improvement workflows
  • Leadership, supervision, and partner coordination pathways

Careers, Not Jobs

DSGE is structured around long-term capability building. The goal is not just to fill positions, but to create visible advancement pathways tied to operational discipline, technology fluency, field readiness, and leadership development.

  • Entry pathway: field support, sorting, documentation, logistics assistance
  • Skilled pathway: equipment, quality, recovery coordination, reporting support
  • Technical pathway: data systems, workflow automation, traceability support, R&D assistance
  • Leadership pathway: site lead, deployment coordinator, partner manager, program supervisor

Required Staffing Structure

Staffing requirements scale by deployment size, distance, material complexity, and partner coverage. A baseline production-ready structure may include:

  • Executive / program lead
  • Operations and deployment coordinator
  • Field technicians / operators
  • Logistics and material flow support
  • ESG / reporting / documentation support
  • Partner development and account coordination
  • Technology / data / dashboard support
  • Safety, quality, and compliance oversight

R&D, University, and VoTech Partnership Pathways

DSGE is designed to work with universities, community colleges, vocational schools, workforce boards, makerspaces, technical programs, and applied research partners to strengthen talent development and innovation capacity.

  • University partnerships for research, validation, and applied analytics
  • VoTech and trade pathways for operator training and equipment readiness
  • Community college pathways for logistics, maintenance, and supervisory skills
  • Workforce development partnerships for recruitment, certification, and advancement
  • R&D collaboration for process improvement, material recovery methods, and pilot validation
